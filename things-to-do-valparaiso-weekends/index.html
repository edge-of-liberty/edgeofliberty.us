Things to Do in Valparaiso This Weekend

Skip the parking headaches and explore the good stuff in the middle

North Valpo and South Duneland Are Having a Moment

If you're searching for things to do in Valparaiso this weekend, you do not have to fight for parking downtown or join the shoreline traffic headed toward the beach. Some of the best weekend wandering is happening in the middle: the north end of Valparaiso, the south end of Duneland, and the country roads around Four Corners.

On select craft fair Sundays, start with The Edge of Liberty Craft Fair: handmade goods, local artisans, fresh food, music, farm gardens, and a relaxed outdoor setting just north of town.

Then keep exploring. Within just a few miles, you can find nearby markets, Four Corners Winery, Rogers Lakewood Park, scenic back roads, and one of Northwest Indiana's classic drive-in theaters.

As Liberty Township looks toward its bicentennial, this old crossroads is becoming what it once was: a place where neighbors meet, visitors linger, and community grows.

Quick note: The places below are not formal partners or paid recommendations. They are local stops we are happy to point visitors toward because a stronger, more connected Valparaiso area helps everyone.

The Sign at Gospel Corners

If you have driven past The Edge of Liberty, you may have noticed the old Bible verse sign at the corner. We are not a church, and as far as we know, no church ever stood on this property.

When we bought the land in 2016, the area around the sign was so overgrown that we could not even see both sides of it. We still do not know who painted it or when it was first put up, but neighbors remember it being here for generations. In 2024, donated funds from our customers helped us build a new steel frame so the sign could keep standing.

What we have learned is that this crossroads was once commonly known as Gospel Corners, named for a small Methodist Episcopal church that stood nearby in the 1800s. The church is gone, but the old name still feels like a clue about what this place can be: not a church, but a neighborly gathering place rooted in care, memory, and community.

That is the spirit we are trying to preserve: a rural Liberty Township place where people can gather on Sundays, celebrate life, create happiness, and help keep local history visible.

Stay in the Middle

Use this as a choose-your-own-weekend list for the overlooked sweet spot between downtown Valpo and the beach. Check each location's current hours, event calendar, tickets, and weather updates before heading out.

This is the Four Corners area where Liberty, Center, Washington, and Jackson townships come together. It is close enough to feel connected, but spread out enough to breathe.

Markets, Music & More

  • Coffee Creek Farmers Market - A south Duneland market stop to pair with a north Valpo Sunday.
  • Chesterton's European Market - A bigger Saturday market option if you are making a full weekend of it.
  • Valparaiso Market - The downtown farmers market exception: useful for fresh food and morning errands before heading north.
  • The Edge of Liberty Craft Fair - Our select-Sunday outdoor market with handmade goods, local vendors, food, music, garden walks, and free admission and parking.
  • The Sunday Vibe Market - A nearby Sunday market stop for vintage finds, plants, handmade goods, and local shopping energy.

Explore the Outdoors

  • Moraine Nature Preserve - A quiet nature stop north of Valpo.
  • Coffee Creek Preserve - Trails, water, and wildlife habitat in south Duneland.
  • Zona Wildlife Sanctuary - A nearby protected natural area for people who want something calmer than the shoreline rush.
  • Shirley Heinze Land Trust - The local land trust behind many protected natural places in Northwest Indiana.

Weekend Spots

  • Sunset Hill Farm County Park - A north-side park stop that keeps you out of downtown and away from beach traffic.
  • Rogers Lakewood Park - A 122-acre north-side park with trails, open water, fishing, kayaking, disc golf, playgrounds, shelters, and room to breathe.
  • Four Corners Winery - A country winery and event spot near the crossroads of Liberty, Center, Washington, and Jackson townships. Watch their calendar for food events, live music, and festival weekends.
  • 49'er Drive-In Theatre - A classic Valparaiso drive-in movie theater just up the road, perfect for turning a Sunday outing into a full weekend.

Build a Weekend Without the Crowds

  • Come Saturday night for a movie at the 49'er Drive-In.
  • Stay nearby and wake up close to the north Valpo and south Duneland loop.
  • Shop handmade goods, plants, vintage finds, and local food at nearby Sunday markets.
  • Have lunch by the pond or at a winery event instead of waiting in a downtown line.
  • Spend the afternoon at Four Corners Winery, Rogers Lakewood Park, or a scenic back-road drive.
  • Head home without fighting beach traffic or packed shoreline parking lots.

A Simple Four Corners Route

Saturday night: Catch a movie at the 49'er Drive-In, then stay close enough that Sunday starts easy.

Sunday morning: Visit The Edge of Liberty Craft Fair, walk the garden paths, meet local vendors, and pick up something handmade.

Midday: Add The Sunday Vibe Market or lunch at the craft fair, then wander the country roads around Four Corners.

Afternoon: Head to Four Corners Winery, Rogers Lakewood Park, or another nearby country-road stop.

Evening: Roll home from the middle, not from the traffic jam.

We have ten years until Liberty Township's bicentennial, and we would love to see this corner become one of the places people remember when they tell that story.
